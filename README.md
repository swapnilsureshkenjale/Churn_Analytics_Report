# 📊 Customer Churn Analysis

Welcome to the repository for my **Customer Churn Analysis** project. This analysis explores key factors influencing customer attrition, focusing on demographic segmentation, tenure distribution, account information, and behavioral correlations.

## 📋 Project Overview

Understanding why customers leave is critical for business sustainability. This project utilizes data visualization and statistical summaries to identify trends across various customer segments. 

### 🏆 High-Level KPIs
Based on the dashboard analysis, the current state of the customer base is as follows:
* **Total Customers:** 6,418
* **Total Churn:** 1,732
* **New Joiners:** 411
* **Overall Churn Rate:** 27.0%

---

## 🔑 Key Findings

### General Insights
* **Segment Dominance:** The **>50 age group** consistently represents the largest segment (2.8K customers), accounting for over 43% of total customers, and carries a high churn rate of 31.0%.
* **Tenure Correlation:** There is a strong relationship between the volume of customers and churn rates across specified tenure groups.
* **Gender Disparity:** The data indicates that female customers exhibit a higher total churn (64.15%) compared to male customers (35.85%).
* **Contract & Payment:** Customers on **Month-to-Month contracts** churn at a significantly higher rate (46.5%) compared to One-Year (11.0%) or Two-Year (2.7%) contracts. Mailed checks also correlate with a higher churn rate (37.8%).
* **Geographic Peaks:** Jammu & Kashmir currently experiences the highest state-level churn rate at 57.2%.

### Demographic Breakdown

### Reason for Churn
The highest driver for customer churn is **Competition** (761 customers), followed by Attitude (301) and Dissatisfaction (300).

## 📈 Methodology

This analysis segments the customer base into five distinct tenure groups (<6 Months, 6-12 Months, 12-18 Months, 18-24 Months, >=24 Months). Across these groups, we observed:

* **Customer Volume:** Ranged between roughly 1K (12-18 months) and 2.1K (>=24 months).
* **Churn Rate:** Remained relatively consistent across the mid-tenure groups, ranging between 26.1% and 27.5%.
* **Divergence:** The gap between total customer volume and churn rate is widest in the **>= 24 months** tenure category, indicating a critical window for retention efforts.
